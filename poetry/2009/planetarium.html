Planetarium

A swath of the moon’s surface hangs
in the dusk, a fruit
the air is swollen
enough to bear.

The sun’s angle brightens the sky
from the inside, like eyelids,
the planet a jewel as always but
only now giving that special
angular wink.

The walls come close enough
for an echo, but still if I speak softly
my words don’t come back,
stay warm in my ear.

The novel of the world
becomes a short story

and the sun is really setting on
its broader themes, invisible
to its protagonist, felt only
as some kind of gravity, the sense

of falling
around a star.
Knowing it is already eluding
him again.
