What was that melody

In the hall between karaoke rooms
the musical undertow:
it will do
for silence.

To my softened mind
every gesture of ours
fills with warm consequence;
every flit of my eyes
a song on someone’s balcony,
if you’re listening.
We hop between established melody
and improvised bends.

This thought keeps us from parting just yet:
whatever note you make the last
becomes perfect.
Becomes the whole.
