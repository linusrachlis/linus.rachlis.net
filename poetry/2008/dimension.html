Dimension

A little refraction sharpens
those layers of trees
into precise retinal shadowplay.

The branches waver minutely, each in a glass slide
and project their shimmers on the ground.
It’s been too long since I last
ran into the picture.

At first, as the plane
approaches in the greater, empty distance
it simply slides, like a paper plane
that a child is dragging straight along the wall.

Then, without moving,
I notice it’s actually carving through
obliquely
the sound trailing
a few seconds behind it,
the sound of a skyscraper of graphite
drawn smoothly across a mountain face,
echoing
though only empty sky lies behind.

It stabs through the world
coming toward me
then rising away
and so does every person and car,
unable to move without violence,
tearing the canvas.

I sit frozen in place
parallel
to nothing.
