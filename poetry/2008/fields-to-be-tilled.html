Fields to be Tilled

If what’s attractive are contradictions
        —changes in direction
      surprising harmonies—

must I choose between two kinds of love?

The one that hangs
on the balance of who I am,
    narrower and narrower;
the other
that creates the balance?

Who could fall in love with a monk,
who has already given himself up,
the last blood running
from his heart like warm water,
ready to rejoin the sea?

Desire requires lives ahead,
fields to be tilled,
time to be filled:

undiscovered
countries.
