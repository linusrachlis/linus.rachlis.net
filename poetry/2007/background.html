Background

We have a huge umbrella.
The park is full and roaming,
the air thick and fresh,
         the bright grey of
            sunlight through bedsheets.

    A globe of falling drops
        the swish-swish of raincoats
      our breaths under the umbrella
  cars leaving slow streaks on the streets.

The brimming atmosphere faithfully
   wraps itself
      ‘round the curves of
      each word and sound.

          We could float away
                          if the ground
                                let go.
