Empty Rooms

Bang.
Is always followed by a period.

It’s only a decision the first time:
a new colour on your hands
afraid the stars will sing you to hell

but soon it soaks through your wrists
your arms—a tree
     with the time-earned right to reach out:
     those standing beneath you
     dread any breeze;
soon you descend red-faced and fear no hell—
                                                         you provide it.

The trigger becomes part of you like a boot heel
stomping blindly
on a pack of sick dogs.

Those left standing
in the rooms that aren’t empty
keep licking, like a prayer,
at that raw little corner
      that never quite stays down
      when you smooth it out
but the wind has already picked up.

The feeling of what you’ve done
drains from you early,
hardens into a reflex in your finger,
hardens your ears.

As they yelp
a soft bell rings.
