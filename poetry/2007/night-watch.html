Night Watch

So much must conspire to rest this body and mind.
Cocktail hour: the darkness circulates.
A potion, grudgingly taken, tumbling limbs;
the mind, a heavy beast, lays down its horns.

Takes its nightly intermission inside
a pool of absence neither black nor white,
wraps itself up tight and waits, open—
the water rises quick from under noticing.

Insulated, electrolysed mind,
deserted as its army buoys free,
can breathe underwater in its dreams,
touch all its moments
             there among the seaweed.

Old captain having sunk unto the depths,
next stands, dons the cap,
             skims the letter that was left.
