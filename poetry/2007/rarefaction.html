Rarefaction

I feel religious
sometimes
on aeroplanes.

It’s the urgent take-off
pounding and pounding
pure passion winning out
for once

then afterwards the closeness to space
a hundred pairs of feet
tip-toeing around the edge of reality.
             I imagine that
             God notices
             each step
so I must notice, too.

What story will I tell
             of these high cotton fields?
Of ice threading across the window
ears popping in the expanding space
the explosions on which we ride
             smoothing out behind us?

What sort of story is here, the wings barely swaying
             in the sun as I sit, sipping?
             Close the window shade.
We’re losing time.

And close the lingering space between:
touch my face
tell me I’m still here.

Touch-down:
just a little kiss.
